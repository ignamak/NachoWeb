Making games is something personal, it's the perfect combination
of art and technology, it's a cultural product and it has to be done responsibly.
Making them while having fun is my passion.

Over twenty years ago I won a Chinese NES clone console in a raffle, to this day, I have not stopped thinking of anything other than videogames. Many gamedevs compelling stories tell how they started programming at 12 years old and how they were sucked into the world of development naturally. This is not that story, mine is a story of struggling against the people who told me that this is not a real job and should do anything else. Living up to other people's expectations brought me to three college drop-outs and a depression. Finally, I understood that nobody could live for me and stood up. Got back again to University even though I was "too old" to start again and finish my studies, this time on something I felt right, videogames.

Now I am set in the quest of unravel every inch of the mystery behind the curtain. I am hungry for learning every aspect of game making and the more I learn, the more clear my path is, Game design and programming are my weapons of choice.
